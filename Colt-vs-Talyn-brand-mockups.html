Colt vs Talyn

Two directions, one system. Both built on the LSKD playbook: a single heavy wordmark, a near-monochrome base, one disciplined accent, and confident athletic type. Nothing decorative — the product and the squad do the talking. Below: wordmark treatments, a monogram, the palette, and how the brand lands inside the app's gamification layer (Ladder · Caps · Tiers · XP · Squad · Heat).

How to read these.  Same minimalist system, two personalities. Colt is the bolder, more sport-native swing — the accent and attitude carry it, and it earns instant meaning (the junior grade). Talyn is the safer, fully ownable platform brand — cooler, more “tech”, already clean on the register. Swap either accent (acid → cobalt, or try a rust/coral) without touching the type system. Fonts shown are free stand-ins (Anton + Archivo + Oswald); a paid condensed grotesk would tighten it further at production.
